## How to Become Stronger  

You grow only through experience.  

Otherwise, your world remains imaginary—the world you scroll past in short videos—not the one you truly feel, touch, and navigate.  

Especially experiences that cause short-term pain tend to accelerate growth most noticeably.  

### How Muscles Grow  

Muscles grow not by staying intact—but by being *slightly torn*, then given time to recover and rebuild stronger.  

This process unfolds in four stages:  

1. **Fiber disruption**: During resistance training, microscopic tears occur in muscle fibers.  
2. **Inflammatory response**: The body releases cytokines and growth factors, summoning repair cells to the site.  
3. **Repair and recovery**: Satellite cells fuse with damaged fibers, repairing and thickening them.  
4. **Adaptation and hypertrophy**: Rebuilt fibers become larger and stronger—an adaptive response to future load.  

Repeated cycles of stress + recovery = progressive strength gain.  

The same logic applies to mental strength: enduring setbacks, solving them, moving past them—that *is* training. Each recovery phase deepens resilience. Over time, you don’t just survive hardship—you metabolize it into capacity.

## The Sales Masters  

I visited an education-focused sales company founded by a veteran who’s been in the sector for decades.  

Their operating model is simple but potent: use their own low-barrier “traffic products” (e.g., free assessments, trial classes) to attract highly qualified leads—and then deploy a tightly trained sales team to match those leads with partner institutions’ premium offerings.  

Their core advantage? *Sales excellence, engineered and scaled.*  

The founder shared examples: their reps routinely spend 2–3 hours in a single 1:1 call with a parent—discussing family dynamics, child development, learning habits, even household routines. That depth of human connection isn’t incidental; it’s the engine of conversion.  

Every salesperson is stage-ready—polished, empathetic, tenacious—and most have 5+ years of frontline experience. Walking into their office, you feel it: a palpable energy of disciplined hustle.  

Result? High per-rep conversion rates *and* sustainable scale.  

Today, products are abundant. What’s scarce is people who can *sell*—truly understand needs, articulate value, and close with integrity.  

Those who master both product *and* persuasion? They’re unstoppable.  

## The Power of Focus  

A friend recently visited our office and shared his struggle.  

His company’s revenue has grown steadily—but so has his exhaustion. Physically drained. Mentally frayed.  

Why? Too many business lines—too many directions at once.  

He offered a vivid analogy:  

> “Starting a company is like playing a video game. The winning strategy is to pick *one* game you love *and* that matters—and dive in deeply: study its rules, grind its levels, learn from every boss fight, until you become a top-tier player.  
>   
> What most founders do instead is install *dozens* of games on their phone—then try to play each one just enough to beat the first few enemies. But every game has its own logic, controls, and hidden mechanics. You never get fluent. You never master anything. And in the end? You’re tired—and still stuck at level 5.”  

I couldn’t agree more.  

## How to Think More Rationally  

The simplest, most direct way to train rational thinking? *Stretch your time horizon.*  

Often, just lengthening your mental timeline shifts your entire perspective—even without conscious effort, you slide into rational mode.  

Take running: viewed only through today’s lens—or this week’s—it feels optional, inconvenient, even pointless.  

But hold it in your mind for a year: picture consistent energy, better sleep, sharper focus, fewer sick days, deeper calm. Suddenly, lacing up *now* carries weight. It becomes a choice rooted in consequence—not impulse.  

Rational thinking means weighing short-term cost against long-term payoff—and factoring in second- and third-order effects. It’s not about ignoring emotion; it’s about letting time clarify what truly matters.
